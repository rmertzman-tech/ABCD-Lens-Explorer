The ABCD Framework

While SAGE gives you a systematic method for ethical analysis (See‚ÜíAnalyze‚ÜíGenerate‚ÜíEngage), ABCD provides four fundamental dimensions through which to examine any situation involving human agents.

Think of ABCD as a lens system: you examine the same situation through multiple perspectives simultaneously, revealing patterns and possibilities that single-lens analysis misses. These four dimensions operate at every scale‚Äîfrom cellular biology to global institutions.

The Four Dimensions: Agency (who can act?), Beauty/Awe (what captures meaningful attention?), Context (what conditions shape possibility?), and Dynamics (how does this evolve over time?). Each dimension reveals essential aspects of human coordination and capability.

Agency

Who can act? What expands or contracts capability?

Agency is distributed and nested‚Äîfrom cells to institutions. It's not heroic will but distributed control that can be strengthened or weakened by state (physiological, emotional, social) and structure (institutional design, coalition networks, resources).

Context / Contingency

What conditions shape what's possible?

Context isn't fixed backdrop‚Äîit's designable infrastructure. Standards, timing windows, reception networks, and guardrails expand or constrain possibility space. The same agents produce different outcomes under different contextual conditions.

Dynamics

How does this evolve through time?

Feedbacks, thresholds, attractors, and hysteresis (new set-points that persist). Change isn't linear‚Äîit's state-dependent, with tipping points and stable basins. Understanding dynamics reveals when and how systems can shift.
